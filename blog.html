!HAPPY NEW YEAR TO Y'ALL! Alright, time for those good ol' resolutions...

Day 4:

After the Holidays, it was a bit hard to go back to programming. I will not lie, even though it is fun to code and developp your knowledge, it can sometimes feel like a bit streneous. Doing the Mimo classes is a great way to get started, however I would like to have more "hands on" experience. I feel like a great way to learn is actually by teaching yourself through experiences, projects.
As of early January 2018, I haven't done a New Year's resolutions list, but some impactful decisions were made.

DECISION 1: I want to continue in the right direction: becoming a web developper. For this I came to the conclusion, that following online classes is great, but learning through a bootcamp would be better. After looking nights and days for a meaningful bootcamp, that offers knowledge, a certification, and job opportunity (internships), I found one: Coding Academy by Epitech. Even though their Code & Go program looks awesome, there is a little problem, it is located in France :(

DECISION 2: If, for a lot of people, this would be a deal breaker, it isn't one for my wife and I. Actually I am pretty excited. I applied for the program and I am still waiting to hear back from them, but moving to Paris for 5 months (program length) wouldn't be so bad at all. Since I can remember, my wife has always wanted to live abroad. She did live in Mexico during her studies, but France would be something totally different. Although we have been to Europe a few times (my entire side of the family lives there), we have been leaving/working in the Bay Area for a little over 6 years now, and I am not sure Paris will be anything close to the Bay Area.
